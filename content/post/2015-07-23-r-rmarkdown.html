Introduction

In order to analyze the impact of COVID-19 on our society, the Toronto Public Health has been collecting COVID-19 cases data in Toronto since the first confirmed COVID-19 case in January, 2020. We want to know which group is been affected the most as COVID-19 continues to threaten more lives. In a total of 17872 cases, 52.7% of them are composed by female patients, and 45.8% of them are male patients. From the data, we observed that COVID-19 has an bigger impact on Toronto female residents than male residents. Additionally, the impact of COVID-19 to an individual also differs across different age groups. If the level of impact is defined by whether the patient has been to ICU, we can observe that patients over 50 years old are more likely to develop serious illness, and patients with the age group of 70 to 79 years old have the highest ICU admission rate. In conclusion, COVID-19 has the most impact on females and develops serious among people over 50 years old.

About the Data

The dataset I used is downloaded through the Toronto Open Data Portal which contains all the confirmed and probable COVID-19 cases managed by the Toronto Public Health. The latest update by the time of this project is on 23, September, 2020. The dataset is been refreshed and overwritten every Wednesday. It forms as a table which contains 18 variables related to demographic, geographic, and severity information. Each row represents an independent patient with all the associated information. The database contains 17872 rows in total until Sep 23, 2020. CITATION: Toronto Public Health, Sept 23, 2020. ‘COVID-19 CASES IN TORONTO’ https://open.toronto.ca/dataset/covid-19-cases-in-toronto/

Client Gender is the self-reported gender and generally based on people’s assigned biological sex.

Age Group is the patient’s age at the time of illness , in years. This variable contains 10 elements which are 19 and younger, 20 to 29 Years, 30 to 39 Years, 40 to 49 Years, 50 to 59 Years, 60 to 69 Years, 70 to 79 Years, 80 to 89 Years, 90 and older, and unknown(blank).

Ever in ICU indicates whether if cases were admitted to the intensive care unit (ICU) related to their COVID-19 infection, it includes cases are currently in ICU and those been discharged or deceased.

Discuission – Why female?

From the data collected from 17872 Toronto COVID patients, we can observe that females are being affected the most by COVID-19. There’s an observable difference between the number of male and female patients which is composed by 45.8% and 52.7% of the total cases population. One potential reason for higher infection rate is that females tend to have a higher participation rate working in service industries(Kwolek-Folland, A., & Walsh, M. 2007), such as restaurents and healthcare facilities etc. Citation: Kwolek-Folland, A., & Walsh, M. (2007). Women in the Service Industries: National Perspectives. The Business History Review, 81(3), 421-427. Retrieved September 27, 2020, from http://www.jstor.org/stable/25097375

Without the influence of blink data in age group, I expect to see ICU proportion and age group have a positive relationship, that is, ICU proportion rate will increase as age increases.The scatterplot describes that people with age group 70-79 years has the highest ICU proportion which is around 0.0794, and people with age group 20-29 years has the lowest ICU proportion which is around 0.0043. However,people over 79 years old have a low ICU proportion especially for the group 90 and older, with only 0.005%.

Discuission – Why COVID hurt 70-79 years old so much? Weakness and potential missing data.

According to Professor Hassan Vally, he noticed human’s immune system weakens as we age. In the graph above I only analyzed if the patient has ever been sent to ICU without the consideration of death rate. We observed the ICU proportion starts to increase for those over 50 years old, then there’s a significant decrease for people over 79 years of age. However, the death rate begins to increase for people over 50 years old and reaches the peak at over 90 years old age group. The cruel reality is that many elderly people living alone have passed away without being noticed at home, they can be accounted to missing data. Another thing causing the misleading ICU proportion is for those old people who are sent to hospital can’t take their last breath before transfering to ICU due to their weak immune system.
